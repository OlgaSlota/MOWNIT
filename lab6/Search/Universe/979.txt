Time Warner companies decided to drop the word "Inc.", including DC Comics. January[edit] Adventures of Captain America #4, the final issue of the limited series (Marvel) February[edit] Captain Confederacy vol. 2, #4, the final issue of the limited series (Epic Comics) March[edit] Batman #475: Introduction of Renee Montoya. Hook #4, the final issue of the bi-weekly mini-series (Marvel) April[edit] The Amazing Spider-Man #361: First full appearance of Carnage. May[edit] Captain America #400: 80-page giant; double gatefold cover; flip-book with a reprint of Avengers #4 (Silver Age re-introduction of Captain America) on the opposite side. Uncanny X-Men #300: 68-page giant featuring "Legacies," by Scott Lobdell, John Romita Jr., and Dan Green. Namor the Sub-Mariner #26 (May cover date) – Jae Lee makes his Marvel Comics' debut. July[edit] Amazing Heroes, the long-running industry magazine published by Fantagraphics, folds after 203 issues. Introduction of James Rhodes as War Machine in Iron Man #282. Adventures of the Thing #4, the final issue of the mini-series featuring reprints from Marvel Two-In-One (Marvel) Defenders of Dynatron City #6, the final issue of the video game adaptation (Marvel) August[edit] Savage Sword of Conan #200: "Barbarians of the Border," by Roy Thomas, John Buscema, and Ernie Chan. The Amazing Spider-Man #365: Special 30th Anniversary issue with a hologram on the cover. A backup story, "I Remember Gwen" drawn by John Romita The Pirates of Dark Water #9, the final issue of the Hanna-Barbera fantasy animated series adaptation (Marvel) September[edit] With #37, Legends of the Dark Knight changes its title to Batman: Legends of the Dark Knight. Cops: The Job #4, the final issue of the mini-series (Marvel) October[edit] October 9: The Words & Pictures Museum of Fine Sequential Art, founded by Kevin Eastman, opens its doors in Northampton, Massachusetts Batman: Sword of Azrael #1: Introduction of Azrael, who would later become Batman during the Knightfall storyline. Dinosaurs, a Celebration #4, the final issue of the mini-series (Marvel) Warlock vol. 2, #6, the final issue of the limited series (Marvel) November[edit] Cable — Blood and Metal #2, the final issue of the limited series (Marvel) Infinity War #6, the final issue of the limited series (Marvel) December[edit] The Incredible Hulk reaches issue #400 (December cover date). The Hulk battles the Leader. The Punisher/Captain America: Blood and Glory #3, the final issue of the limited series (Marvel) Deaths[edit] March[edit] March 12: Hans G. Kresse, creator of Eric de Noorman, dies at age 70. June[edit] June 3: William Gaines, publisher of EC Comics and Mad magazine, dies at age 70. June 6: Martin Goodman, long-time Marvel Comics publisher, dies at age 84. June 11: Todd Loren, writer, editor, and publisher of Revolutionary Comics, is murdered at aged 32. July[edit] July 12: Al Gabriele dies at age 75. July 30: Superman co-creator Joe Shuster dies at age 78. August[edit] August 26: Bob de Moor, long time collaborator of Hergé and creator of Barelli and Cori, de Scheepsjongen, passes away at age 66. December[edit] December 24: The Smurfs creator Peyo dies at age 64. Unknown date[edit] Silver Age letterer Sam Rosen dies. Exhibitions and shows[edit] September 13–November 7: (The Ohio State University Martin Luther King Jr. Complex for Performing and Cultural Arts, Columbus, Ohio) — Political Satire by Oliver Harrington (part of the Festival of Cartoon Art) — examples of Harrington's comic strip character Bootsie, as well as his magazine cartoons October 9, 1992 – January 5, 1993: Words & Pictures Museum (Northampton, Massachusetts) grand opening exhibition, featuring Mike Ploog, Jack Kirby, Bill Sienkiewicz, Paul Mavrides, Gilbert Shelton, John Severin, Mark Martin, and Wally Wood October 26–December 11: (The Ohio State University Main Library, Philip Sills Exhibition Hall, Columbus, Ohio) — Illusions: Ethnicity in American Cartoon Art (part of the Festival of Cartoon Art) — examination of racial and ethnic stereotypes in cartoons from the past 150 years Conventions[edit] January: CAPTION (Oxford Union Society, Oxford, England, U.K.) – inaugural event January 4–5: Great Eastern Comic Book Convention[1] (Holiday Inn, St. Louis, Missouri)[2] January 23–26: Angoulême International Comics Festival (Angoulême, France)[3] March 14–15: GlasCAC (Candleriggs Town Hall, Glasgow) — 3rd annual Glasgow edition of the UKCAC; official guests include creators from the "Deadline Mini-Tour": Nick Abadzis, Rachel Ball, Philip Bond, Glyn Dillon, D'Israeli, Evan Dorkin, Jamie Hewlett, Alan Martin, Shaky 2000, and Si Spencer April 12: International Seattle Comic Convention (e.g., "Center Con") (Seattle, Washington) – guests include Martin Nodell[4] May 9: Ramapo Comic Con VI (Ramapo High School, Spring Valley, New York) — guests include Brian Augustyn, Rick Bryant, Mike Carlin, Dave Cockrum, Denys Cowan, Evan Dorkin, David Chelsea, Ken Gale, Ron Garney, Jack C.
