January[edit] January 4: Axel Alonso is named editor-in-chief of Marvel Comics, replacing Joe Quesada.[1][2] March[edit] Catawiki, an online compendium of collector's catalogues, becomes available in English. July[edit] July 19: Stage show Batman Live premiers in Manchester Evening News Arena August[edit] As part of their The New 52 publishing scheme, DC Comics cancels all their ongoing titles. September[edit] During the month, DC Comics releases 52 brand-new titles as part of their The New 52 publishing scheme. Deaths[edit] February[edit] February 21: Dwayne McDuffie, co-founder of Milestone Media, dies at age 49. June[edit] June 23: Gene Colan, long-time artist for Marvel Comics, died at age 84. August[edit] August 18: Jean Tabary, creator of Iznogoud, died at age 81. September[edit] September 4: Dave Hoover dies at age 56. September 16: Tom Wilson, creator of the comic strip Ziggy, dies at age 80. September 18: Jack Adler, long-time member of the production staff at DC Comics, dies at age 94. October[edit] October 31: Mick Anglo, creator of Marvelman, dies at age 95. November[edit] November 8: Bil Keane, creator of The Family Circus, died at age 89. December[edit] December 7: Jerry Robinson, creator of the Joker, dies at age 89.[3] December 14: Joe Simon, co-creator with Jack Kirby of Captain America, dies at age 98 after a brief illness.[4] Conventions[edit] March 4–6: Emerald City ComiCon (Washington State Convention & Trade Center, Seattle, Washington, USA) — 32,000 attendees; guests: Frank Quitely, Jonathan Hickman, Max Brooks, Geof Darrow, Steve Epting, Jacen Burrows, Guy Davis, Terry & Rachel Dodson, Mike McKone, Sergio Aragonés, Dan Jurgens, Marv Wolfman, Cameron Stewart, John Arcudi, Yanick Paquette, Bruce Timm, Brian Michael Bendis, Frank Cho, J.
