Bongo Comics drops the Comics Code Authority seal. February[edit] February 18: Jim Lee and Dan DiDio announced as the new co-publishers of DC Comics, replacing Paul Levitz. July[edit] July 18: Publication of the first issue of the Kodansha shōjo manga magazine Aria. September[edit] September 9: The play Cheapjack Shakespeare, based on the comic of the same nam, debuts in Buffalo, New York. December[edit] DC Comics shuts down its WildStorm imprint. Deaths[edit] January[edit] January 3: Tibet (aka Gilbert Gascard), creator of Chick Bill, dies at age 78 January 3: Barry Blair of Aircel Comics passes away at age 55 January 21: Jacques Martin, creator of The Adventures of Alix dies at age 88 January 25: Bill Ritchie, creator of Baby Crockett and Sweet Sue, passes away at age 78 February[edit] February 26: Violet Barclay, pioneering female comics artist, dies at age 87 March[edit] March 19: John Hicklenton passes away at age 42 after suffering from multiple sclerosis March 27: Dick Giordano dies at age 77 April[edit] April 4: Henry Scarpelli passes away at the age of 79. April 27: Kees Kousemaker, founder of the first comic book store in the world, Lambiek, dies at age 68. May[edit] May 3: Peter O'Donnell, the creator of Modesty Blaise, dies at the age of 90 May 10: Frank Frazetta passes away, aged 82 May 16: Philippe Bertrand, aged 61[2] May 22: Howard Post dies, aged 83 June[edit] June 2: Tony DiPreta dies aged 88 June 2: Antonio Parras, aged 81[3] June 12: Al Williamson, Eisner Award-winning inker, dies at age 79. July[edit] July 12: Harvey Pekar, autobiographical comics pioneer and writer of American Splendor, dies at age 70. September[edit] September 10: Paul Conrad, political cartoonist, dies at age 86.
