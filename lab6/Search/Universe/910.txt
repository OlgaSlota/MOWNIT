October 15: first publication of Little Nemo in Slumberland by McCay in New York Herald.[9] Unknown date: a collection of McCay's Dreams of the Rarebit Fiend strips is published by the Frederick A. Stokes Company of New York. 1908[edit] Harold R. Heaton joins the Inter-Ocean newspaper as an editorial cartoonist. 1909[edit] U.S. publications[edit] November 1: first appearance of The Umbrella Man by John Hager for the Seattle Daily Times, appearing on the front page as a supplement for the weather.[10] Not titled as the Umbrella Man, but called that May 3, 1913 under a section called "Features of Today's Paper".
