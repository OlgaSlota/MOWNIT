Fawcett Publications is settled out of court; Fawcett agrees to quit using the Captain Marvel character(s) and pay DC Comics the sum of $400,000.[1] Subsequently, Fawcett leaves the comics publishing business. Tut le Blanc's comic strip An Altar Boy Named Speck begins syndication March[edit] Al Gordon's art career begins with Trojan Comics' Attack! #6 and Beware #14. April[edit] Master Comics, with issue #133, canceled by Fawcett. June[edit] Whiz Comics, with issue #155, canceled by Fawcett. Captain Marvel Jr., with issue #118, canceled by Fawcett. November[edit] Captain Marvel Adventures (1941 series), with issue #150, canceled by Fawcett Comics. Hopalong Cassidy, with issue #85, canceled by Fawcett Comics. Two-Gun Kid (1948 series), with issue #11, revived by Marvel.
