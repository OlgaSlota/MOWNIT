In addition, David Anthony Kraft's Comics Interview publishes its first comic book titles (it had been publishing the Comics Interview magazine since 1983). After 41 years as a publisher, Charlton Comics folds. Marvel Comics publishes Heroes for Hope: Starring the X-Men, an all-star benefit book for African famine relief and recovery. January[edit] Warrior, with issue #26, publishes its final issue (Quality Communications) February[edit] Tales of the Teen Titans #50: Donna Troy marries Terry Long. (DC Comics) With issue #150, Marvel Comics cancels the Spider-Man title Marvel Team-Up (Web of Spider-Man will debut two months later). March[edit] Peter Parker, the Spectacular Spider-Man #100: "Breakin'!" written and drawn by Al Milgrom. "The Surtur Saga" comes to a conclusion in Thor #353 by Walt Simonson. April[edit] Crisis on Infinite Earths 12-issue "maxi-series" debuts, produced by DC Comics to simplify their then-50-year-old continuity. Written by Marv Wolfman, and illustrated by George Pérez (pencils/layouts),[1] with Mike DeCarlo, Dick Giordano, and Jerry Ordway (who shared inking/embellishing chores). The series eliminates the concept of the Multiverse in the fictional DC Universe, and depicts the deaths of such long-standing superheroes as Supergirl and the Barry Allen incarnation of the Flash. May[edit] Sgt. Rock #400: "Easy's 400th," by Robert Kanigher and Adrian Gonzales. (DC Comics) Summer[edit] Marvel Comics publishes New Mutants Special Edition #1. Written by Chris Claremont, it is the first X-Men related art by fan-favorite Art Adams with inks by Terry Austin. It continues in Uncanny X-Men Annual #9 again by Claremont and Adams with inks by Al Gordon, Mike Mignola, and Adams, himself. July[edit] Secret Wars II debuts continuing the adventures of the Beyonder from the original Secret Wars series. August[edit] DC publishes Superman Annual #11: "For the Man Who Has Everything," by the future Watchmen team of Alan Moore and Dave Gibbons. September[edit] The "Who Is Sensor Girl?" storyline, written by Paul Levitz, begins in Legion of Super-Heroes #14 (and continues through issue #27). Mighty Crusaders vol. 2, with issue #13, is cancelled by Archie Adventure Series. October[edit] Crisis on Infinite Earths #7: The death of Supergirl. With issue #350, DC Comics publishes the final issue of The Flash (volume 1). "The Death of Jean DeWolff" story arc, written by Peter David, begins in Peter Parker, the Spectacular Spider-Man #107 (eventually running through issue #110). Music for Mechanics, the first Love and Rockets collection, is published by Fantagraphics. November[edit] Crisis on Infinite Earths #8: The death of Barry Allen / The Flash. (DC Comics) With issue #50, Arak, Son of Thunder (1981 series) is canceled by DC Comics. Tales of the Teen Titans, with issue #59, becomes a reprint book. (DC Comics) Iron Man #200: "Resolutions!" by Denny O'Neil, Mark D. Bright, and Akin & Garvey. Tony Stark resumes the identity of Iron Man and defeats Iron Monger. (Marvel Comics) December[edit] The Warlord #100: double-sized issue, "Skartaris Unchained," by Michael Fleisher and Adam Kubert. (DC Comics) Uncanny X-Men #200: "The Trial of Magneto," by Chris Claremont, John Romita, Jr., and Dan Green. (Marvel Comics) Thundercats #1: "Survival Run". (Star Comics) December 31: The Dreamer, by Will Eisner, is published by Kitchen Sink. Deaths[edit] March[edit] Writer Don Rico, known for his jungle girl characters, dies at age 72. May[edit] May 11: Chester Gould, creator of Dick Tracy, dies at age 84. June[edit] June 21: Charles Wojtkoski, creator of Blue Beetle, dies at age 63. July[edit] Ernie Hart, creator of Super Rabbit, dies at age 64. July 8: British cartoonist Frank Hampson dies at age 66.
